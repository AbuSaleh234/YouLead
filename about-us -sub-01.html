Why YouLead Africa

Amplify Youth Voices in Key Regional Political Governance Institutions & Processes and Accelerate Regional Integration

For African governments, development partners, private sector and other key actors, taking proactive measures to include young leaders in decision-making associated with key development policies and processes should be observed as an indicator of success on the Human Development Index. Through our regular Youth Governance Architecture Studies, we conduct evidence based participatory research assessing the status of youth participation and create platforms that facilitate gender responsive, youth-centered social dialogues between youth leaders, policy makers and development partners. We advise governments, development partners, private sector and other key actors on best models and ways to enable meaningful participation of African youth in key governance and development institutions and processes.

Enhance Capacity of Youth in Business, Trade & Enterprise to effectively Pursue Economic Opportunities & Decent Work

With 65% of Africa's population being below the age of 35 years, Africa is the world’s youngest continent. This trajectory presents both opportunities and challenges for the continent’s peace and development agenda - estimates indicate that half of Africa’s young people are at the risk of not securing decent employment or access to means of production and the associated economic opportunities.
In pursuit of Growth and Economic opportunities for youth, YouLead seeks to contribute toward advocating for an enabling environment for youth-led national and cross-border entrepreneurship, business and enterprise to flourish while supporting them to create business solutions that results in decent work and economic opportunities for more youth.
We seek to empower and position youth to utilize the existing business friendly policies and opportunities especially within the Framework of Regional Economic Commissions (RECs) and more recently the African Continental free Trade Area Agreement (AfCFTA).

Human Capital for Africa’s Transformation

Africa’s youth demographic dividend offers a human resource potential for a fastened implementation of the African Union Agenda 2063 – the Africa we want and United Nations Agenda 2030 in favour of Africa’s transformation agenda. We seek to ensure that African youth are adequately aware, skilled, mobilised and capacitated to play a role in the implementation of Agenda 2063 and Agenda 2030. We work to prepare African youth to become transformational leaders, efficiently and effectively driving the economic and political development of the continent – making African youth a resource pool well equipped to deliver the future of the continent’s youthful promises as ‘One Young Africa’ in its sophistication and diversity.
