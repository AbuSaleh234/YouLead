Our Parent Organisations

MS Training Centre for Development Cooperation

Situated in Arusha Tanzania, MS TCDC is a Training Centre for Development Cooperation is home of YouLead and jointly hosts the YouLead Secretariat through a Cooperation Agreement with the East African Community. The Cooperation Agreement between MSTCDC and EAC mandates MSTCDC to build the capacity and ensure continued engagement of the EAC Youth Ambassadors Platform in mobilizing and reaching out to youth in all EAC programs and promoting the aspirations for regional integration among youth. The Centre was founded in 1967 on the basis of a bilateral agreement between the governments of Denmark (Royal Danish Government) and Tanzania. MS-TCDC offers technical assistance and capacity building programs for the state and non-state actors in Africa, Asia, Latin America, and the Arab regions to enhance their ability to influence democratic governance, equitable, sustainable, and transformative change. MS TCDC program priority areas are good governance, transformational leadership, gender equality, youth voice, participation, and climate governance.

The East African Community (EAC)

The East African Community (EAC) is a regional intergovernmental organization of six (6) Partner States; Burundi, Kenya, Rwanda, South Sudan, Uganda, and the United Republic of Tanzania. Its vision is a prosperous, competitive, secure, stable and politically united East Africa with a mission of widening and deepening economic, political, social and cultural integration to improve the quality of life of the people of East Africa through increased competitiveness, value-added production and investment

The East African Community Youth Ambassadors Platform

The EAC Youth Ambassadors is an initiative under the Nyerere Centre for Peace Research with the mandate of increasing EAC sensitization among young people in the EAC. This mandate extends to being active influencers of EAC programs and policy frameworks to address the emerging day-to-day realities of young people within the EAC. The EAC Youth Ambassadors Platform was launched in September 2013 under the EAC Secretariat Political Affairs Department to serve as a forum for current and alumni Ambassadors. At the national level, the Ambassadors have worked in close collaboration with Ministries responsible for East African Community Affairs. The Platform brings together both former and serving ambassadors to achieve their mandate. The mandate of the East African Community Youth Ambassadors Platform is derived from Articles 120 (c), 127, 128, 129 (2) of the Treaty for the Establishment of the East African Community which inter alia advocates for and voices the interest of the youth in the East African Community integration process.
